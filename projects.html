Drought Forecast In Pastoral Communities

Forecasting vegetation conditions with satellite Earth Observation datasets using Bayesian/Hierarchical Bayesian Models. The models were trained to forecast the Vegetation Condition Index (VCI) over grasslands that serve as grazing fields for livestock farmers. One of the models from this work has been implemented in decision support tools in Kenya, and forecasts from the model are currently used by institutions like the Kenya Rangeland Trust and Kenya Red Cross for monitoring short-term drought forecasts for early action.

Estimating Peatland CO2 Exchange

Using a combination of CO2 exchange data from a flux tower and high-resolution satellite earth observation data, a model was calibrated to estimate Gross Primary Production (GPP) and Ecosystem Respiration (ER) over peatlands (Bogs) at a spatial scale. This project aimed to monitor restored peatland sites that were previously CO2 sources. Continuous monitoring of the peatland sites with satellite-derived indices will enable us to determine if the restored peatland, known to capture and store CO2, has reverted to its natural state (CO2 sinks).

Mapping Banana Plantations

Using Sentinel 1 (SAR) and Sentinel 2 (Optical) remote sensing data, advanced machine learning models were trained to classify and map banana plantations over Costa Rica. The objective was to map out all plantations to enable near real-time monitoring of banana plants for disease or nutrient deficiency.

Remote Sensing for Water Quality

Developed remote sensing data pipelines and processing chains to estimate water quality products (Chlorophyll-a) using Sentinel 2 and Sentinel 3 image data. The main goal of this project was to produce weekly maps to monitor Cyano-Bacteria Blooms in in-land lakes.

Urban Tree Mapping

Develop deep learning (computer vision) models for identifying trees in urban areas in Stirling, Scotland. A Region-Based Convolutional Neural Network (R-CNN) based on a Retina-Net architecture was trained and fine- tuned using very high-resolution aerial image data. The aim here was to identify and quantify trees in urban areas. For densely wooded areas and tree clumps, a semantic segmentation deep learning model based on the U- net architecture was also trained to mask and approximate the number of trees in dense woodlands.
